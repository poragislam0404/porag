I'm a PhD candidate at Stanford's AI Lab, advised by Prof. Li Fei-Fei. My research sits at the intersection of computer vision, natural language processing, and human-computer interaction — specifically, how machines can understand and communicate nuanced visual concepts.

Before Stanford, I spent two years at Figma building real-time collaboration infrastructure, and a summer at NVIDIA Research working on neural rendering pipelines. I've shipped code used by millions and published at top-tier venues including NeurIPS, CVPR, and ICLR.

I care deeply about systems that work at scale — both in production and in research. I believe good engineering and rigorous science aren't opposites; the best work lives at their intersection.
